# Stretch Goal: Custom Google Fonts 🔤

Help the attendee pick and apply custom Google Fonts to personalize their website's typography.

## What to Do

1. Read the existing `index.html` and `style.css`
2. Ask the attendee what vibe they want for their fonts (or suggest options)
3. Update the Google Fonts `<link>` in `index.html`
4. Update the `font-family` declarations in `style.css`

## Font Pairing Suggestions

Offer these curated pairings:

### 🎀 Elegant & Sophisticated
- Heading: **Playfair Display** (serif)
- Body: **Lato** (sans-serif)

### 💪 Bold & Modern
- Heading: **Space Grotesk** (sans-serif)
- Body: **Inter** (sans-serif)

### 🌸 Soft & Friendly
- Heading: **Quicksand** (sans-serif)
- Body: **Nunito** (sans-serif)

### ✍️ Handwritten & Personal
- Heading: **Caveat** (handwriting)
- Body: **DM Sans** (sans-serif)

### 🏛️ Classic & Timeless
- Heading: **Cormorant Garamond** (serif)
- Body: **Source Sans Pro** (sans-serif)

## Implementation

1. Replace the existing Google Fonts `<link>` tag in `<head>` with the new fonts
2. Update `font-family` for headings (`h1, h2, h3`) and body text in `style.css`
3. Adjust `font-weight` if needed for the new fonts
4. Check that the site still looks good — adjust `font-size` or `line-height` if the new fonts feel too big/small

## Constraints
- **Only use Google Fonts** (free, no licensing issues)
- **Keep it to 2 fonts max** (one heading, one body) for performance
- **Preserve readability** — body font must be highly legible at small sizes
